# Slice of Success — Pizza Sales & Revenue Analytics Dashboard

A Power BI dashboard redesigned from chart dump to business report —
tracking revenue, orders, and product performance for a pizza business since 2015.

---

## The Problem With Version One

The first version had five pie charts, no visual hierarchy, and no narrative flow.
Every chart showed *something*. Nothing told you *anything*.

A viewer landing on that dashboard had to decode it themselves — figure out what
to look at first, what mattered, what to ignore. That's not a dashboard. That's
raw data with colors.

---

## The Redesign

One question drove the entire rebuild: what does a restaurant owner need to read
in under 30 seconds?

That question cut everything that didn't answer it and shaped everything that did.

**Slice of Success** is the result — a single-page report with a clear reading
flow, consistent branding, and visuals that earn their space.

## Dashboard Features

**Revenue Contribution by Category**
Classic leads at 26.91%, followed by Supreme (25.46%), Chicken (23.96%),
and Veggie (23.68%). Relatively balanced — no single category dominates.

**Top 10 Revenue Generating Pizzas**
The Thai Chicken Pizza sits at the top. Bar chart format makes the ranking
immediately readable, unlike the pie chart version that required decoding
percentages across five slices.

**Peak Ordering Hours**
Order volume spikes around 11AM–2PM and holds through the evening.
Useful for staffing and promotional timing decisions.

**Revenue by Pizza Size**
Large pizzas drive the most revenue by a significant margin, followed by
Medium and Small. XL and XXL are marginal contributors.

---

## Before vs After

**Before**
Five pie charts. Scattered KPIs with inconsistent sizing. No title, no branding,
no logical reading order. Data is present — story is absent.

**After**
Branded header. Four KPI cards at the top. Structured sections below with
consistent chart types and a flow that guides the viewer from summary to detail.

## Key Takeaway

new ones. The tool is secondary to the thinking behind it.
